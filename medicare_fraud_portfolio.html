The Problem

Fraud at Scale

Medicare processes hundreds of billions in claims annually, making it a prime target for fraud, waste, and abuse. With 691,000+ active providers, traditional manual auditing cannot keep up.

  • Fraudulent billing hides within normal-looking claim patterns over time
  • Random or complaint-driven auditing misses systemic fraud for years
  • No single metric can reveal fraud — multi-dimensional analysis is required
  • Investigators lack a data-driven system to prioritize where to look first
✅
The Solution

Data-Driven Risk Scoring

An automated anomaly detection pipeline that scores every Medicare provider against their specialty and state peer group — turning 13.6M rows of raw claims data into a prioritized audit list.

  • Peer benchmarking by specialty + state for fair comparison
  • 6 fraud indicators calculated per provider from raw billing data
  • Z-score outlier detection flags statistically abnormal billing behavior
  • Weighted composite risk score ranks all 691K+ providers 0–100
How It Works

The 6-Stage Pipeline

01
Data Ingestion
Loaded 13.6M+ rows of CMS Medicare Part B & Part D data into MySQL, resolving formatting errors and currency inconsistencies.
02
Peer Groups
Grouped each provider into a peer cohort by specialty type and state — ensuring apples-to-apples comparison.
03
Feature Engineering
Calculated 6 fraud indicators per provider: services per beneficiary, payment per service, charge ratio, drug cost, claims rate, and cost per claim.
04
Z-Score Analysis
Measured how many standard deviations each provider's metrics fall above their peer group. Z ≥ 3 triggers a fraud flag.
05
Risk Scoring
Combined all 6 Z-scores into a weighted composite fraud risk score from 0–100, classifying providers into High, Medium, and Low tiers.
06
Dashboard
Built a 5-page interactive Power BI dashboard enabling investigators to explore results by state, specialty, and individual provider.

Methodology

The Risk Scoring Model

// Composite Fraud Risk Score (0–100)
// Z-scores capped at 5 to prevent single outliers dominating

Risk Score =
  MIN(5, |z_services_per_bene|) / 5 × 20 +
  MIN(5, |z_payment_per_service|) / 5 × 20 +
  MIN(5, |z_charge_to_payment|) / 5 × 10 +
  MIN(5, |z_drug_cost_per_bene|) / 5 × 20 +
  MIN(5, |z_claims_per_bene|) / 5 × 20 +
  MIN(5, |z_drug_cost_per_claim|) / 5 × 10

// Maximum possible score: 100

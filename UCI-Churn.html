Key Findings

What the Data Revealed

Six data-backed findings uncovered through segmentation analysis across job type, age, education, loan status, campaign timing and contact frequency.

01

Campaign Fatigue

Churn rate rises consistently with every additional contact — reaching 95% at 7+ contacts. The bank's best retention window is the very first call.

95% churn at 7+ contacts vs 87% at 1 contact
02

Previous Campaign Success is the Strongest Predictor

Customers whose previous campaign resulted in success churn at just 35% — compared to 91% for customers never previously contacted.

Previous success → 35% churn vs 91% baseline
03

Seasonal Churn Pattern

May alone accounts for 33% of all churn. The May–August period drives nearly 78% of total churn — yet this is when the bank runs its heaviest campaigns.

May = 33% of all churn · Sep–Mar = lowest risk
04

Age & Job Risk Concentration

45-54 and 35-44 year olds churn at 91%. Blue-collar and services workers churn at 93% and 92%. Together these groups dominate the high-risk pool.

Top 3 jobs = 65% of all churn
05

Contact Method Gap

Customers contacted by telephone churn at 95% vs 85% for cellular — a 10 percentage point difference suggesting mobile outreach is significantly more effective.

Telephone 95% vs Cellular 85% churn
06

Highest Risk Segment Identified

Housemaid + basic.9y education customers churn at 96.81%. Self-employed + basic.4y at 96.77%. Lower educated, non-salaried workers are the hardest to retain.

Top segment: Housemaid + Basic 9y = 96.81%

Business Recommendations

From Data to Action

Five specific, data-backed recommendations directly tied to findings — each with a clear rationale and expected business impact.

# Recommendation Data Rationale Expected Impact Priority
01 Hard cap all campaigns at 3 contacts per customer Churn exceeds 92% after 3 contacts — every additional call wastes budget Reduce campaign cost by 30–40% Critical
02 Prioritise re-engagement of previous campaign successes Previous success reduces churn from 91% to 35% — 3x better conversion 3x improvement in conversion rate Critical
03 Shift campaign timing to September–March Sep–Mar accounts for only 22% of churn vs 78% in May–August 30–40% lower churn during campaign period High
04 Switch outreach from telephone to cellular contact Cellular churn (85%) is 10 points lower than telephone (95%) 10% improvement in retention per contact High
05 Focus acquisition campaigns on student & retired segments Students (69%) and retired (75%) have lowest churn of any job group 20–25% better campaign ROI Medium

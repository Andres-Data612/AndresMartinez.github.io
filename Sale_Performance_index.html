Discount Impact & Margin Leakage
Transactions bucketed by discount rate (0–5%, 5–10%, 10–20%, 20–30%, 30–45%) to quantify how much gross margin is lost at each tier. Formula: Revenue Lost = Quantity × (List Price − Sale Price). Margin Lost = Revenue Lost × GM%.
Finding: Discounts above 20% account for $547K — 68% of the total $798K margin leakage
Product Profitability & Category Mix
All 25 products analyzed by revenue, COGS, gross margin %, and units sold. A GAP column (% of Margin − % of Revenue) surfaces which categories punch above or below their revenue weight. Electronics flagged as a margin drag with an 8-point negative gap.
Finding: Laptop Pro 15 at 23% GM% and Robot Vacuum at 26% GM% are the lowest-margin products in the portfolio
Sales Rep Performance by Region
All 15 reps ranked by gross margin dollars, not just revenue to separate genuine high performers from discount-heavy closers. A Revenue Efficiency score (GM$ ÷ Target$) was added. Conditional formatting flags reps more than 10% above or below target in green and red.
Finding: Southeast reps Frank Dunn and Elena Voss both run 36% GM%, the lowest in the company
Monthly Revenue & Margin Trend
Jan–Dec revenue and gross margin plotted as a dual-line chart to surface seasonality. May recorded the highest revenue month at $892K. Gross margin % improves in Q4-December hits 41% suggesting reduced discounting behaviour when reps are under quota pressure at year-end.
Finding: Replicating Q4 discount discipline year-round could add 2–3pp to annual company-wide GM%
Key Findings
Critical
$798K in margin leakage detected
Discounts above 20% account for $547K of total leakage 68% of the total. The 30–45% bucket had 806 orders with no approval ceiling. Implementing a 15% cap could recover $300K–$500K annually.
Product
Electronics is the biggest margin drag
Electronics drives 39% of revenue but only 31% of gross margin, an 8-point gap. The Laptop Pro 15 (23% GM%) and Robot Vacuum (26% GM%) are primary culprits due to high COGS and aggressive field discounting.
Regional
Southeast underperformed target by 10%
The worst regional performance. Frank Dunn leads Southeast in revenue but carries only 36% GM%, suggesting discount-led selling behaviour rather than value based selling.
Seasonality
May spike and Q4 margin recovery identified
May hit $892K peak revenue while April was the weakest at $523K. GM% improves to 41% in December — reps discount less when under year-end quota pressure. This pattern should be replicated earlier in the year.
Performance
All 15 reps missed revenue target
Company wide miss of 7% against a $9.1M target. A systemic miss across every region and rep signals a target setting methodology problem, not a sales execution problem.
Recommendations
High Priority
Implement 15% discount cap
Require manager approval for any discount above 15%. The 30–45% bucket had 806 orders with zero ceiling in place of a governance gap.
↗ Recover $300K–$500K in annual margin
High Priority
Reprice Laptop Pro 15
At 23% GM% on $1.8M in revenue, this is the single largest margin leak in the product portfolio. Reprice or renegotiate supplier COGS.
↗ Potential $200K+ margin recovery
Medium
Reset targets bottom-up
A 100% miss rate across all reps signals a target-setting problem, not a sales execution problem. Use rolling 3-year actuals as the baseline.
↗ Improved rep morale and retention
Medium
Shift mix toward Food & Sports
Food & Beverage runs 58% GM% and Sports averages 52% GM%. Growing these categories lifts overall margin without discounting reform.
↗ +2–3pp overall GM% improvement
